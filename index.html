F1 GP Breakdown

Race Recap

Hungarian Grand Prix (July 20, 2025) — A tense duel between the McLarens kept Budapest buzzing. Lando Norris converted an early undercut into the lead, but Oscar Piastri hounded him all the way to the line, finishing just 0.7 seconds adrift. George Russell brought the Mercedes home P3 after a clever one‑stop, holding off late pressure from Charles Leclerc. Key moment: McLaren split their tyre strategy on lap 18, forcing Red Bull’s hand and leaving Max Verstappen stuck in traffic. Fastest lap went to Piastri, sealing a McLaren 1–2 and tightening the fight in the constructors’ standings.

Tech Talk: Belgian Grand Prix — Spa‑Francorchamps

Circuit overview

  • Name: Circuit de Spa‑Francorchamps
  • Length: 7.004 km (longest on the calendar)
  • Laps: 44 (race distance ~308.052 km)
  • First F1 GP: 1950
  • Character: Long straights, high‑speed corners, big elevation; Ardennes micro‑climate can split the track between wet and dry.

Technical and tyre notes

  • Downforce: Medium–low to protect top speed on Kemmel while keeping Sector 2 stable.
  • Tyres: Typically mid‑range compounds (C2–C4); cool, damp air can cause graining.
  • Strategy: One or two stops viable; Safety Cars and weather often swing the call.
  • Power unit & cooling: High full‑throttle time; brake energy moderate but cooling is critical.

Weather watch

  • Temperatures: High‑teens to low‑20s °C are common.
  • Rain risk: Sudden showers are notorious; Intermediates often decide margins.

Sections to spotlight

  • Eau Rouge/Raidillon: Compression to crest at full chat; balance drag vs. front‑end bite.
  • Kemmel Straight: Primary DRS and overtaking zone; tow effect is strong.
  • Les Combes–Malmedy: Vulnerable to switchbacks if the defender over-commits.
  • Pouhon: Sustained lateral load; left‑side tyre temps spike.
  • Blanchimont: Commitment‑heavy, especially in dirty air late in stints.
  • Bus Stop chicane: Heavy braking; traction crucial onto the main straight.
